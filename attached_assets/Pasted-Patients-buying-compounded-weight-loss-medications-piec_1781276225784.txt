Patients buying compounded weight-loss medications piece together pricing from Reddit threads and outdated pharmacy listings. Prices swing from $200 to $600 per vial for the same concentration. Compounding regulations shift by jurisdiction and change without warning. Half the telehealth providers advertising online operate in legal gray zones that look identical to the legitimate ones. Buyers want safe, affordable medication. Instead they get a comparison nightmare.

Pepcheck is a comparison platform that shows real-time pricing, vial concentrations, and jurisdiction-level legality for vetted telehealth pharmacies selling compounded GLP-1 medications. A patient picks a region, compares options side by side, reads verified reviews, and confirms the provider operates legally where they live. Provider listings update when shortage rules or compounding regulations change. Telehealth providers pay for placement. Patients get qualified options checked for legality and pricing accuracy before they commit.

Start with an Airtable backend listing 12 major telehealth providers with their pricing, concentrations, and regional availability. Run a lightweight scraper over provider sites and regulatory feeds weekly. Build a frontend in Webflow where users filter by location and sort by price. Add a basic review system using Typeform and manually verify submissions before publishing. Ship it to compounded medication communities as a free beta. Watch which filters people use and which questions they ask before adding features.

Providers pay $500 a month for featured placement or $50 per qualified lead. Premium features like legality alerts, price-drop notifications, and insurance lookups add a subscription layer. White-label versions let clinics run the same comparison for their own patients. Insurers steer members toward compliant providers, pushing the platform past direct-to-consumer. Compounded weight-loss medications are the entry point. Hormone therapy and peptides slot into the same price-and-legality table. A patient who finds a vetted, legal option at a fair price stops refreshing Reddit and places the order.
